Yardi for Build-to-Rent: What Irish Property Firms Need to Know

Ireland's Build-to-Rent sector is maturing fast. Here is how Yardi equips Irish property firms to manage large residential portfolios with the operational precision the market now demands.

Artisan Solutions 6 min read Market Insights

Ireland's Build-to-Rent sector has established itself as a significant part of the country's residential landscape. Dublin, Cork, and Galway are home to a growing number of large-scale, professionally managed apartment communities backed by institutional capital. The regulatory environment has evolved substantially in recent years, with rent pressure zones now extended nationwide as of June 2025 and a new national rent control framework in place from March 2026. For BTR operators, navigating this environment while delivering a high standard of resident experience and institutional-grade reporting requires operational infrastructure that is up to the task. Yardi provides exactly that.

For Irish property firms managing BTR portfolios, Yardi brings something that patchwork systems and spreadsheet-based operations simply cannot: a single environment where leasing, operations, finance, and reporting all speak to each other in real time. The result is a portfolio that runs more smoothly, a team that spends less time on administration, and investors who receive the data they need without a week of manual preparation beforehand.

Here is what Yardi specifically offers for the Irish BTR market, and how firms are using it to manage large residential portfolios with the precision the market now demands.

01
Capability One

A single platform for the full residential lifecycle

BTR management involves a wide range of operational activity: marketing vacant units, managing leads, processing applications, executing leases, collecting rent, managing maintenance, handling renewals, and reporting performance to investors. In many Irish property firms, these functions are handled by different systems that do not talk to each other. Yardi brings them all into one environment, which means a leasing event in one part of the system instantly updates the financial picture, the occupancy dashboard, and the investor report without anyone needing to re-enter data.

This integration is particularly valuable in a BTR context where portfolio scale means that manual data transfers between systems create a meaningful volume of administrative overhead. With Yardi, the operational team, the finance team, and the asset management team are all working from the same real-time picture of the portfolio.

02
Capability Two

Lease management built for the Irish residential market

Irish residential tenancy law has undergone significant changes in recent years. From June 2025, rent pressure zone rules were extended to cover all private tenancies nationwide. From March 2026, a new national rent control framework replaced the RPZ system, introducing six-year minimum tenancies for new tenancies, stricter restrictions on no-fault terminations, and updated rules on rent setting. New-build apartments with planning notices submitted after June 2025 are exempt from the 2% cap, with increases instead tied to the Consumer Price Index, a change designed to encourage new rental supply.

Yardi's lease management capability can be configured to reflect these requirements directly, so that compliance is built into the workflow rather than depending on individual team members to apply the right rules manually every time. For BTR operators managing hundreds or thousands of tenancies simultaneously, this is transformative. Rent review dates, notice period calculations, and RTB registration reminders can all be automated within the system. The team focuses on managing relationships with tenants rather than tracking compliance deadlines across a spreadsheet.

Yardi gives Irish BTR operators something that no combination of separate systems can replicate: a single source of truth for every unit, every tenancy, every payment, and every investor report. That is what operational excellence at scale actually looks like.

03
Capability Three

Resident-facing technology that raises the tenant experience

BTR in Ireland is a product as much as it is a service. Tenants in professionally managed apartment communities expect to be able to pay rent online, submit maintenance requests through an app, communicate with the management team digitally, and receive timely responses. Yardi's RentCafe platform delivers exactly this, giving residents a branded digital portal that handles payments, communications, and service requests in one place.

For the management team, RentCafe reduces the volume of inbound calls and emails, creates a documented trail of every interaction, and feeds all activity back into the core Yardi environment automatically. Maintenance requests raised through the portal become work orders in the system. Payments processed through the portal update the rent ledger in real time. The tenant experience improves, and the operational team has more time to focus on the work that actually requires human judgment.

04
Capability Four

Investor reporting that meets institutional expectations

The capital behind Ireland's BTR market is predominantly institutional. REITs, pension funds, and international real estate investors all have sophisticated reporting expectations. They want fund-level performance metrics, asset-level detail, occupancy analysis, collections data, and net effective rent calculations in formats that are consistent, timely, and comparable across periods. Yardi's reporting engine, properly configured, delivers all of this directly from the system without requiring a team to rebuild the numbers in a spreadsheet before every distribution.

Custom reports built around each investor's specific requirements mean that the right data goes to the right audience in the right format. Quarterly investor packs that previously took a finance team several days to assemble can be produced in a fraction of the time, with greater consistency and with the confidence that comes from knowing the numbers came directly from the system of record.

05
Capability Five

Scalability as the portfolio grows

One of the most valuable things about Yardi for Irish BTR operators is that it scales with the portfolio. Adding a new development does not require a new system. Onboarding a new property means configuring it within the existing Yardi environment, applying the same reporting structures, the same charge codes, the same workflows, and the same tenant-facing portal that the rest of the portfolio already uses. The operational team does not need to learn a new tool. The finance team does not need to integrate a new data source. The investor receives reporting in exactly the same format as before, just with more units in it.

For firms with ambitious growth plans in the Irish market, this scalability is a genuine competitive advantage. The infrastructure that supports a 200-unit portfolio today can support a 2,000-unit portfolio tomorrow without a fundamental rebuild of how the organization operates.

Getting started

What Irish BTR firms should prioritize in their Yardi setup

Getting the most from Yardi in an Irish BTR context means configuring the platform around the specific requirements of the Irish residential market from the start. The following priorities have the highest impact on how well the system serves the organization as the portfolio grows.

  • Configure lease structures to reflect Ireland's current national rent control framework, including the March 2026 rules on minimum tenancy duration, rent setting, and RTB registration workflows
  • Set up RentCafe as the resident-facing portal from day one so that digital communication and payment become the default from the first tenancy
  • Build investor reporting templates around the specific metrics and formats your institutional investors expect, rather than adapting standard Yardi reports afterward
  • Establish a consistent property and unit coding structure from the outset so that new developments can be onboarded cleanly without disrupting existing reporting
  • Configure automated reminders for rent reviews, lease expiries, and RTB registration deadlines so that compliance is managed by the system rather than by individual team members
  • Integrate maintenance workflows with the resident portal so that work orders, scheduling, and completion are tracked end to end within Yardi
